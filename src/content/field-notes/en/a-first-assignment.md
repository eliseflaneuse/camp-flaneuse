The Society is not open yet, but assignments do not wait for institutions.

So here is the first one, issued informally, before there is anyone to issue it properly:

**Assignment No. 000 — The Unremarkable Walk**

Take a walk you have taken many times before. The boring one. To the shop, around the block, from the car park to the door. Walk it slower than necessary — slow enough to be slightly embarrassing.

Your task is to find, on this walk you know by heart, **one thing you have never noticed before**. A crack shaped like a river delta. A window that does not match its building. A plant committing trespass. The way a particular gate has been repaired three different ways by three different hands.

That is the whole assignment. You do not need to write it down, though writing it down counts as extra credit at an institution that does not exist yet and therefore cannot grade you.

What you find is yours. What the finding does to the walk — that is the Society's actual curriculum, and the only membership requirement we will ever check.
