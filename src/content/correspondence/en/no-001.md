To whoever has found the light on,

Some places begin by putting up walls. Camp Flaneuse began by leaving a light on.

This is the first letter from its correspondence desk. The desk is still being built. There are not enough drawers, the stamps have not been chosen, and a deer is delaying approval of the official seal. Still, a first letter has to begin somewhere.

Camp Flaneuse is a moving place for field notes, drawings, letters, strange sightings, and the slow construction of a freer life. It is made from a motorhome named Ember, a few cats, too many notebooks, and the belief that attention is not a decorative quality. It is one of the ways we remain alive.

The Soft Footsteps Society is also being founded here. It is a society for attentive walkers, creature-noticers, letter-keepers, and people trying to move through the world without crushing every small thing beneath them.

Enrollment is not open yet. Nobody has decided what enrollment means.

But you may consider this an invitation.

Enclosed—in the peculiar way that something can be enclosed on the internet—is Part I of *The Deer Ghost*. It is not a report. It did not happen exactly. It may still be true.

Read it slowly. Stories are living things, and some of them become frightened when handled too quickly.

Before the next letter, notice one thing that would have disappeared if you had been in a hurry. You do not need to understand it. Write it down anyway.

The camp has room for it.

Until next time, walk softly.

— **Elai**
*at the correspondence desk*
