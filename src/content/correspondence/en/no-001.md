Dear wanderer,

Some notes are not posts. Some things arrive better as letters, and this is the first one, so forgive it for being slightly ceremonial. First letters always are.

I want to tell you about the deer.

A few weeks ago, at dusk, on a small road I had no particular reason to be on, a deer stepped out of the tree line and stood there — not startled, not posing, just *present*, the way a thought is present before you have words for it. We looked at each other for longer than these things usually last. Then it walked back into the trees, unhurried, as if it had confirmed something and could now continue.

I have not stopped thinking about it. Not because I believe it meant anything, exactly. But because I noticed that I *wanted* it to mean something, and that wanting felt important. The soul, I suspect, sometimes knows the path before the body arrives. The deer was standing on mine.

*[A drawing of the deer will live here, once the deer agrees to be drawn.]*

This camp — the notes, the letters, the whole small institution — is an attempt to take that kind of noticing seriously. To treat attention as a practice, the way other people treat running or prayer. Not to extract meaning from everything, but to be *available* to it. There is a difference, and I am still learning it.

So here is the invitation, since a first letter should contain one:

Pay attention to something this week that has no use to you. A bird you can't name. A building that is losing gracefully to ivy. The specific color of the evening you are in. You do not have to write it down. But if you do, you are already — informally, unofficially, softly — one of us.

More letters will follow. There is talk of a society. There is a professor involved, allegedly. The details are still walking here.

From the road, with attention,

**Elai**
